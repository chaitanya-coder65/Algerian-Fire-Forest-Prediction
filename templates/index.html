System Documentation

Before using the predictor, understand the meteorological parameters that drive our Ridge Regression model.

Temperature & RH Ambient air temperature and Relative Humidity. High heat and low humidity dry out forest fuels, making them highly flammable.
Ws (Wind Speed) Wind supplies oxygen to fire and helps it spread faster. High Ws values exponentially increase the fire spread rate.
FFMC (Fine Fuel Moisture Code) Represents the moisture content of litter and other cured fine fuels. It indicates how easily a fire can start.
DMC (Duff Moisture Code) Represents the moisture of loosely compacted organic layers. It helps predict the consumption of fuel in moderate-depth layers.
ISI (Initial Spread Index) A numerical rating of the expected rate of fire spread. It combines the effects of wind and FFMC.
FWI (Fire Weather Index) The final score. It is a general index of fire intensity, used to determine the overall danger level of the forest.
